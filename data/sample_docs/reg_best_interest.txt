Regulation Best Interest (Reg BI): Standards of Conduct for Broker-Dealers

SECURITIES AND EXCHANGE COMMISSION
17 CFR Part 240 — Rule 15l-1
Effective Date: June 30, 2020

OVERVIEW

Regulation Best Interest (Reg BI) establishes a new standard of conduct for broker-dealers and their associated persons when making recommendations to retail customers of any securities transaction or investment strategy involving securities. The rule requires broker-dealers to act in the best interest of the retail customer at the time the recommendation is made, without placing the broker-dealer's financial or other interests ahead of the customer's interests.

Reg BI was adopted as part of a broader package of rulemakings, including Form CRS (Client Relationship Summary), which requires broker-dealers and investment advisers to provide a brief summary of their services, fees, and conflicts of interest.

THE FOUR COMPONENT OBLIGATIONS

Obligation 1: Disclosure Obligation
Before or at the time of the recommendation, the broker-dealer must provide the retail customer with full and fair disclosure of:
- All material facts relating to the scope and terms of the relationship, including:
  - That the broker-dealer is acting in a broker-dealer capacity
  - Material fees and costs the customer will incur
  - The type and scope of services provided
- All material facts relating to conflicts of interest associated with the recommendation

The disclosure must be made in writing and delivered before or at the time of the recommendation.

Obligation 2: Care Obligation
The broker-dealer must exercise reasonable diligence, care, and skill when making recommendations. This requires:
- Understanding the potential risks, rewards, and costs of the recommendation
- Having a reasonable basis to believe the recommendation is in the best interest of the particular retail customer, based on the customer's investment profile
- Having a reasonable basis to believe that a series of recommended transactions, taken together, are not excessive and are in the customer's best interest

The customer's investment profile includes: age, other investments, financial situation and needs, tax status, investment objectives, investment experience, investment time horizon, liquidity needs, risk tolerance, and any other information disclosed by the customer.

Obligation 3: Conflict of Interest Obligation
The broker-dealer must establish, maintain, and enforce written policies and procedures reasonably designed to:
- Identify and at a minimum disclose all conflicts of interest associated with recommendations
- Identify and mitigate conflicts that create an incentive to place the broker-dealer's interest ahead of the customer's interest
- Identify and eliminate sales contests, sales quotas, bonuses, and non-cash compensation based on the sale of specific securities or specific types of securities within a limited time period

Obligation 4: Compliance Obligation
The broker-dealer must establish, maintain, and enforce written policies and procedures reasonably designed to achieve compliance with Reg BI as a whole.

FORM CRS (CLIENT RELATIONSHIP SUMMARY)

Both broker-dealers and investment advisers must deliver Form CRS to retail investors. The form is a brief (maximum 2 pages for single registrants, 4 pages for dual registrants) summary that includes:
- Introduction describing the firm and type of services
- Relationships and services offered
- Fees, costs, conflicts, and standard of conduct
- Disciplinary history
- Additional information and how to find it

Form CRS must be filed with the SEC via EDGAR and posted on the firm's website.

COMPARISON WITH INVESTMENT ADVISER FIDUCIARY DUTY

| Feature | Reg BI (Broker-Dealer) | Fiduciary Duty (Investment Adviser) |
|---------|----------------------|-------------------------------------|
| Standard | Best interest at time of recommendation | Ongoing fiduciary duty |
| Scope | Transaction-specific recommendations | Entire advisory relationship |
| Monitoring | No ongoing monitoring duty (unless agreed) | Ongoing monitoring duty |
| Fees | Typically commission-based | Typically fee-based (AUM, hourly, flat) |
| Account type | Brokerage account | Advisory account |
| Key regulation | Exchange Act Section 15(l) | Advisers Act Sections 206(1), 206(2) |

ENFORCEMENT

The SEC has brought numerous enforcement actions for Reg BI violations since its effective date, including:
- Failure to consider reasonably available alternatives before recommending high-cost products
- Inadequate conflict of interest disclosures
- Recommending complex products (leveraged/inverse ETFs, variable annuities) without a reasonable basis
- Failing to address conflicts created by compensation structures

Penalties include disgorgement, civil penalties, censures, and bars from the industry.

COMPLIANCE BEST PRACTICES

1. Develop comprehensive Reg BI policies and procedures
2. Train registered representatives on all four obligations
3. Implement product-level review processes before allowing recommendations
4. Maintain documentation of the basis for each recommendation
5. Conduct regular surveillance for excessive trading and concentration
6. Review and update conflict of interest inventories at least annually
7. Ensure Form CRS is accurate and delivered timely
8. Implement supervisory procedures specific to complex products
9. Review compensation structures for conflicts
10. Establish escalation procedures for identified conflicts
