PART I

ITEM 1. BUSINESS

Apple Inc. designs, manufactures, and markets smartphones, personal computers, tablets, wearables, and accessories worldwide. The Company also sells a variety of related services.

Products:
iPhone — iPhone 16 lineup launched September 2024, featuring Apple Intelligence (on-device AI), improved cameras, and the A18/A18 Pro chips. iPhone revenue for FY2024: $201.2 billion (approximately 52% of total revenue).

Mac — MacBook Air, MacBook Pro, iMac, Mac mini, Mac Studio, and Mac Pro, powered by Apple silicon (M3, M3 Pro, M3 Max, M3 Ultra, M4 chips). Mac revenue for FY2024: $29.4 billion.

iPad — iPad Pro (M4), iPad Air (M2), iPad (10th generation), iPad mini (A17 Pro). iPad revenue for FY2024: $26.7 billion.

Wearables, Home and Accessories — Apple Watch (Series 10, Ultra 2), AirPods (4th generation, Pro 2, Max), Apple Vision Pro, HomePod, Apple TV, and accessories. Revenue for FY2024: $37.0 billion.

Services:
Apple Services include the App Store, Apple Music, Apple TV+, iCloud, Apple Pay, AppleCare, Apple Arcade, Apple Fitness+, Apple News+, and Apple Card. Services revenue for FY2024: $96.2 billion (approximately 25% of total revenue, highest margin segment).

ITEM 1A. RISK FACTORS

The Company faces significant risks including:

Market and Competition Risks:
- Intense competition in all product categories
- Risk of market saturation in smartphones, particularly in developed markets
- Dependence on new product introductions and transitions
- Competitive pressure from AI-native devices and services
- Risk of commoditization in hardware categories

Supply Chain Risks:
- Concentration of manufacturing in a limited number of countries (primarily China, India, Vietnam)
- Single or limited source suppliers for critical components
- Risks from geopolitical tensions (US-China trade relations, Taiwan Strait)
- Component shortages and supply chain disruptions
- Increasing costs of components and manufacturing

Regulatory and Legal Risks:
- Ongoing antitrust scrutiny globally (EU Digital Markets Act, US DOJ antitrust lawsuit)
- EU Digital Markets Act compliance requirements as designated "gatekeeper" for iOS, App Store, Safari
- Japan, South Korea, India regulations requiring alternative app distribution
- App Store commission structure facing global regulatory pressure
- Privacy regulations (GDPR, state privacy laws, international data localization requirements)
- Tax audits and assessments in multiple jurisdictions

Technology and AI Risks:
- Rapid evolution of artificial intelligence and machine learning capabilities
- Risk of falling behind competitors in AI integration
- Apple Intelligence adoption and competitive positioning
- Data privacy constraints limiting AI training data availability
- Dependence on third-party AI partnerships (OpenAI for ChatGPT integration)

ITEM 7. MANAGEMENT'S DISCUSSION AND ANALYSIS

Revenue Analysis:
Total revenue increased 2% year-over-year, driven by growth in Services (+13%) and Mac (+2%), partially offset by declines in iPad (-2%) and Wearables (-7%). iPhone revenue was approximately flat year-over-year.

Greater China revenue declined 5% due to increased competition from Huawei and other domestic manufacturers, currency headwinds, and a slower economic recovery. This represents a key strategic concern, as China accounts for approximately 17% of total revenue.

Services continues to be the fastest-growing and highest-margin segment, with over 1 billion paid subscriptions across the Apple ecosystem. Services gross margin was approximately 74%, compared to 37% for Products.

Gross Margin:
Product gross margin expanded to 37.0% from 36.5%, driven by product mix and component cost reductions, partially offset by foreign exchange headwinds. Services gross margin was 74.0%, approximately flat year-over-year.

Operating Expenses:
R&D expense increased 5% to $31.4 billion, primarily reflecting investments in Apple Intelligence, Apple Vision Pro, and semiconductor design. SG&A was flat at $25.0 billion.

Capital Return Program:
The Company returned $110.1 billion to shareholders through $94.9 billion in share repurchases and $15.2 billion in dividends. The Board authorized an additional $110 billion share repurchase program in May 2024.
