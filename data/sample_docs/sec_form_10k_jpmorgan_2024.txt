PART I

ITEM 1. BUSINESS

JPMorgan Chase & Co. is a leading financial services firm based in the United States, with operations worldwide. The Firm is a leader in investment banking, financial services for consumers and small businesses, commercial banking, financial transaction processing, and asset management. JPMorgan Chase had $4.0 trillion in assets and $345 billion in stockholders' equity as of December 31, 2024.

ITEM 7. MANAGEMENT'S DISCUSSION AND ANALYSIS

Revenue Analysis:
Total net revenue increased 11% year-over-year, driven by:
- Net interest income growth of 4%, reflecting higher rates and loan growth, partially offset by deposit migration to higher-yielding products
- Noninterest revenue growth of 24%, driven by record investment banking fees, strong markets revenue, and asset management fee growth from higher AUM

Net Interest Income:
NII of $92.6 billion reflected the continued benefit of higher interest rates on the loan portfolio and investment securities. Net interest margin was approximately 2.6%. The firm benefited from deposit repricing lags, though deposit beta has been increasing.

Provision for Credit Losses:
Provision of $11.2 billion reflected net charge-offs of $9.8 billion and a net reserve build of $1.4 billion, primarily driven by:
- Credit card net charge-offs of $7.2 billion, reflecting normalization from historically low pandemic-era levels
- Commercial real estate reserves of $2.1 billion for the office subsector
- Forward-looking economic scenarios incorporating probability-weighted outcomes

Noninterest Expense:
Expenses of $91.1 billion increased 6%, driven by:
- Compensation expense ($45.2 billion, +5%) reflecting headcount growth in technology and controls
- Technology expense ($16.8 billion, +10%) reflecting continued investments in cloud, AI/ML, and modernization
- FDIC special assessment ($2.9 billion pre-tax) related to the 2023 bank failures
- Regulatory and compliance costs

Capital Management:
- CET1 capital ratio of 15.3% provides a significant buffer above requirements (including G-SIB surcharge)
- Returned $33.4 billion to shareholders through share repurchases ($22.0 billion) and dividends ($11.4 billion)
- Stress Capital Buffer (SCB) of 3.3%

RISK MANAGEMENT

The Firm employs a comprehensive risk management framework:
- Three lines of defense model (business management, risk management and compliance, internal audit)
- Chief Risk Officer reports directly to the CEO and the Board Risk Committee
- Enterprise-wide risk appetite framework with defined limits
- Stress testing (CCAR/DFAST) drives capital planning
- Real-time risk monitoring for market and counterparty risks
- Operational risk management including cybersecurity, technology, and third-party risk

AI and Technology:
JPMorgan Chase is one of the largest technology investors in financial services:
- Annual technology spend: $16.8 billion
- Technology employees: approximately 57,000
- AI/ML use cases in production: 400+ (fraud detection, risk management, customer experience, trading)
- LLM and generative AI pilots in document analysis, code generation, and customer service
- Investments in quantum computing research

REGULATORY CAPITAL

As a G-SIB, JPMorgan Chase faces the highest capital requirements:
- Minimum CET1: 4.5%
- Capital Conservation Buffer: 2.5%
- G-SIB Surcharge: 4.0% (Bucket 4 — highest among US banks)
- Stress Capital Buffer: 3.3%
- Countercyclical Capital Buffer: 0%
- Total CET1 Requirement: 14.3% (effective minimum including all buffers, adjusted for SCB)
- Actual CET1 Ratio: 15.3% (buffer of ~100 bps above requirement)

Resolution Planning:
The Firm's resolution plan (living will) describes how the Firm could be resolved under bankruptcy in a rapid and orderly manner without government support. The plan is updated biennially and has received "no deficiency" findings from the Federal Reserve and FDIC.
