SOC 2 Compliance Requirements for Financial Technology Companies

AMERICAN INSTITUTE OF CERTIFIED PUBLIC ACCOUNTANTS (AICPA)
Trust Services Criteria — SOC 2 Type II Reporting Framework

OVERVIEW

SOC 2 (System and Organization Controls 2) is an auditing framework developed by the AICPA that evaluates an organization's information systems relevant to security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy. SOC 2 reports are critical for financial technology companies that process, store, or transmit customer financial data.

Unlike SOC 1 (which focuses on financial reporting controls), SOC 2 examines operational and compliance controls based on the AICPA Trust Services Criteria (TSC).

TRUST SERVICES CRITERIA

Security (Common Criteria — CC)
The system is protected against unauthorized access, unauthorized disclosure of information, and damage to systems that could compromise availability, integrity, confidentiality, and privacy.

Key requirements:
CC1 — Control Environment: Management demonstrates commitment to integrity and ethical values. The board exercises oversight responsibility. Management establishes structures, authority, and accountability.

CC2 — Communication and Information: The entity obtains and generates relevant quality information. The entity internally communicates information necessary for internal controls. The entity communicates with external parties.

CC3 — Risk Assessment: The entity identifies and assesses risks. The entity assesses fraud risk. The entity identifies and assesses changes that could impact internal controls.

CC4 — Monitoring Activities: The entity selects and develops monitoring activities. The entity evaluates and communicates deficiencies.

CC5 — Control Activities: The entity selects and develops control activities to mitigate risks. The entity deploys IT general controls. Control activities include logical access, physical access, change management, and system operations.

CC6 — Logical and Physical Access Controls:
- CC6.1: Implements access control software, architecture, and procedures
- CC6.2: User registration and authorization for access
- CC6.3: Manages access removal upon termination/role change
- CC6.4: Restricts physical access to facilities and equipment
- CC6.5: Manages data protection for at-rest and in-transit encryption
- CC6.6: Manages system boundaries (firewalls, IDS/IPS, DMZs)
- CC6.7: Restricts data transmission to authorized users
- CC6.8: Prevents and detects unauthorized software

CC7 — System Operations:
- CC7.1: Monitors for anomalies indicating new vulnerabilities
- CC7.2: Monitors system components for known vulnerabilities
- CC7.3: Evaluates security events to determine incidents
- CC7.4: Responds to identified security incidents
- CC7.5: Identifies and mitigates the root cause of incidents

CC8 — Change Management:
- CC8.1: Authorizes, designs, configures, codes, tests, and approves system changes

CC9 — Risk Mitigation:
- CC9.1: Identifies and assesses risk from business partner relationships
- CC9.2: Assesses risk and implements controls for vendor/third-party risks

Availability (A)
The system is available for operation and use as committed or agreed.

A1.1: Maintains, monitors, and evaluates capacity planning
A1.2: Provides for disaster recovery through data backups, recovery procedures, and testing
A1.3: Tests recovery plan procedures

Processing Integrity (PI)
System processing is complete, valid, accurate, timely, and authorized.

PI1.1: Obtains and generates data and information that is complete, accurate, and timely
PI1.2: Implements policies for system inputs to ensure they meet defined specifications
PI1.3: Processes system transactions that are complete, accurate, and timely
PI1.4: Implements policies to address system output completeness and accuracy
PI1.5: Stores and protects system inputs, outputs, and intermediate results

Confidentiality (C)
Information designated as confidential is protected as committed or agreed.

C1.1: Identifies and maintains confidential information
C1.2: Disposes of confidential information to meet entity objectives

Privacy (P)
Personal information is collected, used, retained, disclosed, and disposed of in conformity with privacy commitments and criteria.

P1.1: Provides notice to data subjects about privacy practices
P2.1: Communicates choices available regarding collection, use, and disclosure
P3.1: Collects personal information consistent with notice and choice
P4.1: Uses personal information only for identified purposes
P5.1: Retains personal information only as necessary
P6.1: Discloses personal information only with consent or as agreed
P7.1: Disposes of personal information in accordance with policy
P8.1: Notifies affected parties of unauthorized access or disclosure

FINTECH-SPECIFIC CONSIDERATIONS

Data Encryption Requirements:
- AES-256 encryption for data at rest
- TLS 1.2 or higher for data in transit
- Hardware Security Modules (HSMs) for cryptographic key management
- Key rotation policies (minimum annually, or upon personnel changes)

Multi-Tenancy Controls:
- Logical data separation between clients
- Tenant-specific access controls and audit logging
- Isolation of processing environments
- Separate encryption keys per tenant

API Security:
- OAuth 2.0 / OpenID Connect for authentication
- API rate limiting and throttling
- Input validation and output encoding
- API versioning and deprecation policies
- Comprehensive API audit logging

Payment Card Industry (PCI DSS) Alignment:
SOC 2 controls should align with PCI DSS requirements where the entity processes payment card data:
- Network segmentation (PCI DSS Req. 1)
- Cardholder data protection (PCI DSS Req. 3, 4)
- Vulnerability management (PCI DSS Req. 5, 6)
- Access control (PCI DSS Req. 7, 8, 9)
- Monitoring and testing (PCI DSS Req. 10, 11)
- Information security policies (PCI DSS Req. 12)

AUDIT PROCESS

SOC 2 Type I: Point-in-time assessment of control design (snapshot)
SOC 2 Type II: Evaluation of control design AND operating effectiveness over a period (typically 6-12 months)

Type II is strongly preferred for fintech companies, as it demonstrates sustained compliance rather than a single-day snapshot.

Audit Phases:
1. Readiness Assessment: Gap analysis against TSC criteria
2. Remediation: Address identified gaps in controls
3. Observation Period: Controls must operate effectively for the audit period (minimum 3 months, typically 6-12 months)
4. Fieldwork: CPA firm examines controls, tests samples, interviews staff
5. Report Issuance: CPA firm issues opinion on control effectiveness
6. Continuous Monitoring: Maintain controls and prepare for next audit cycle

COMMON DEFICIENCIES IN FINTECH SOC 2 AUDITS

Based on industry data, the most frequently cited deficiencies include:
1. Incomplete access reviews (quarterly reviews not documented)
2. Missing or outdated risk assessments
3. Inadequate change management documentation
4. Insufficient incident response testing
5. Gaps in vendor risk management
6. Inadequate logging and monitoring (log retention < 1 year)
7. Weak password policies (not enforcing MFA for all access)
8. Missing data classification frameworks
9. Incomplete business continuity/disaster recovery testing
10. Insufficient security awareness training documentation

REGULATORY OVERLAP

Financial technology companies often face overlapping regulatory requirements:
- SOC 2 + SOX (if publicly traded)
- SOC 2 + PCI DSS (if processing payments)
- SOC 2 + GDPR (if serving EU customers)
- SOC 2 + CCPA (if serving California consumers)
- SOC 2 + NYDFS Cybersecurity Regulation (23 NYCRR 500) (if licensed in New York)
- SOC 2 + OCC/FFIEC guidance (if operating as a bank or bank partner)

Efficient control mapping across frameworks reduces audit fatigue and ensures comprehensive coverage.

COST AND TIMELINE CONSIDERATIONS

Typical SOC 2 Type II engagement for a mid-size fintech:
- Readiness assessment: 4-8 weeks
- Remediation: 4-12 weeks (varies significantly)
- Observation period: 3-12 months
- Audit fieldwork: 4-8 weeks
- Report delivery: 2-4 weeks after fieldwork
- Total timeline: 6-18 months for initial certification
- Annual recertification thereafter

Estimated costs:
- Readiness assessment: $15,000-$50,000
- Remediation (tools, processes): $50,000-$200,000
- Audit fees (Type II): $30,000-$100,000
- Ongoing compliance tools: $20,000-$80,000/year
